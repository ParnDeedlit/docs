A **Skill** in GIS Agent is a saved, reusable workflow — the digital capture of a successful GIS process. When you complete a spatial task (such as generating a flood risk map or clipping OSM data to a city boundary), you can save the entire workflow as a Skill. The next time you or a colleague needs the same result, you invoke the Skill instead of re-describing the task from scratch.

## What a Skill Contains

Each saved Skill captures the full context of a completed workflow:

- The sequence of GIS operations performed
- Data source specifications and parameters
- Output configurations (map style, export format, service settings)
- Prompt templates and constraints
- Version history

## Creating a Skill

<Steps>
  <Step title="Complete a task successfully in GIS Agent">
    Run your workflow through to a result you're satisfied with before saving.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save the workflow">
    In the chat interface, type `Save this workflow as a Skill` — or click the **Save as Skill** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Give the Skill a descriptive name">
    Use a name that reflects the business outcome, for example: `Wuhan Flood Risk Map Generator`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add optional metadata">
    Optionally add a description and tags to make the Skill easier to find in your library.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the save">
    The Skill is saved to your account's Skill library and is immediately available for reuse.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Using a Skill

To invoke a saved Skill, you can:

- **Reference it by name** in a new conversation: `Use the 'Wuhan Flood Risk Map Generator' Skill with updated 2024 rainfall data`
- **Select it from the Skill library** in the project sidebar
- **Share it with colleagues** in the same organization (available on enterprise plans)

## Industry Skill Packs

GIS Agent ships with pre-built industry Skill packs for common use cases, so you don't have to build workflows from scratch for well-established tasks:

- **Natural resources:** Land use planning, ecological red-line monitoring
- **Smart city:** Municipal pipeline inspection, community management
- **Energy:** Power grid inspection routing, photovoltaic site selection
- **Emergency management:** Disaster risk warning, resource dispatch mapping
- **Commercial:** Trade area heat analysis, retail site selection

<Note>
Industry Skill availability depends on your subscription plan. Contact MapGIS to enable specific Skill packs for your organization.
</Note>

## Skill Best Practices

<Tip>
- **Name Skills after the business outcome, not the technical process.** Use `Monthly PM2.5 Pollution Map` rather than `Raster clip + WMS publish` — clear names make Skills discoverable and shareable.
- **Archive intermediate results at key checkpoints** using the `Save all parameters and results` command. This protects complex, long-running workflows from being lost mid-process.
- **Review and update Skills when underlying data schemas change.** A Skill that references outdated field names or deprecated data layers will fail silently — periodic review keeps your Skill library healthy.
</Tip>
